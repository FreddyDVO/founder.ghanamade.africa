The Digitizing Africa Project

Office of Interdisciplinary Programs • October 2014 - August 2017

Conducted a multi-disciplinary research in the Republic of Ghana and the Republic of Kenya. The goal of my research was to find viable avenues to improve the efficiency of digital technology for communication and business application on the continent of Africa. I distributed surveys to locals from which I compiled penetration and exposure rates to mobile devices. In addition, I interviewed several government ministers, academics, and business professionals.

From the data and discussions collected, I was able to partner with the National Society of Black Engineers - Ghana, and present my findings at a number of institutions.

Summer Research Intern

NSF-REU: SmartSpaces Project

Department of Computer Science • June 2014 - August 2014

I was a member of a team of 11 engineering students in a federally funded National Science Foundation REU project for 3 months. We were tasked to build a "smart" home environment. My specific contribution to the team's efforts was making the capability of the system to send commands over RF transmission with the use of a Rasberry Pi and Arduino micro-controller. Through this experience, I was introduced to Python, and C#.

Overall the project was geared towards the Android market, which gave us access to open-sourced frameworks and libraries. The only drawback from this experience was the fact Apple came out with their smart home suite of apps and devices only a week into our project's timeline. This significantly took away from team morale to go further with the project after our mandated term.
